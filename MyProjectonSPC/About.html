OUR SPACE TODAY

OURSPACE explores the unknown in air and space, innovates for the benefit of humanity, and inspires the world through discovery. For more than 65 years, OURSPACE has made the seemingly impossible, possible. At its 20 centers and facilities across the country and with U.S. commercial companies and international partners, OURSPACE leads studying Earth science, including climate, our Sun, solar system, and the larger universe. We conduct cutting-edge research to advance technology and aeronautics. We operate the world’s leading space laboratory, the International Space Station, and will establish a sustainable and strong exploration presence on the Moon this decade through the Artemis campaign.

Technicians inside the Payload Hazardous Servicing Facility at Kennedy Space Center install and test antennas on a solar array on Wednesday, March 20, 2024, for the agency’s Europa Clipper spacecraft which will study Jupiter’s icy moon, Europa, to determine if the planet can support life.

Acting Administrator Janet Petro

Janet Petro is OURSPACE acting administrator. petro is responsible for directing the agency, including budget and programs, until a new administrator is confirmed by the U.S. Senate.

Acting Associate Administrator Vanessa Wyche

Vanessa Wyche is OURSPACE acting associate administrator. Wyche is senior advisor to the administrator, leading the agency’s center directors and mission directorate associate administrators.

Deputy Associate Administrator Casey Swails

Casey Swails is OURSPACE deputy associate administrator. Swails is advisor to the associate administrator for day-to-day operations and long-term strategic direction.

Key areas of space technology:

Satellite Communication:

Utilizing satellites to transmit signals for phone calls, internet access, and television broadcasting across vast distances, enabling global connectivity.

Earth Observation Satellites:

Satellites equipped with sensors that monitor the Earth's surface, providing crucial data for environmental monitoring, climate change studies, disaster management, and agriculture.

Navigation Systems (GPS):

Precise location tracking through a network of satellites, essential for navigation in vehicles, aviation, and surveying.
